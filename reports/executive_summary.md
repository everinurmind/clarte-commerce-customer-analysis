# Clarté Commerce — Executive Summary

## Project Overview

Clarté Commerce S.A.S. engaged our team to analyze customer behavior following the Q3 2023 rebanding, which repositioned the brand toward "affordable luxury." The company observed an ~18% decline in repeat purchase rates and needed a data-driven diagnosis.

**Data scope:** 92,310 transactions across 11,572 customers (Jan 2022 – Dec 2024)

---

## Key Findings

### 1. Revenue Impact

Total revenue declined by 20.3% post-rebanding despite a 13.1% increase in average order value (€110 → €125). The higher price point did not compensate for the loss in purchase frequency and customer count.

### 2. Customer Segmentation Shift

RFM analysis revealed significant migration from high-value to at-risk segments:

- **Champions segment shrank by ~23%** (from 17% to 14% of customer base)
- **At Risk segment grew by ~31%** — formerly loyal customers moving to risk zone
- **Lost segment expanded** — silent churn accelerating post-rebrand

### 3. Demographic Patterns

The 35-44 age group — Clarté's core demographic — showed the highest churn rate at 48.5%. This group, predominantly female store shoppers, was most disrupted by the rebanding.

Younger segments (18-24, 25-34) showed stable or slightly improved engagement, suggesting the new positioning resonates with a younger audience but alienates the established base.

### 4. Channel Disruption

Store channel share dropped from 54.4% to 49.7%. Critically, lost store traffic did not migrate to online — it simply disappeared. This indicates the rebrand disrupted the in-store experience without providing a compelling digital alternative.

### 5. Silent Churn

68.6% of churned customers were not opted into email communications. These customers left without any signal — no complaints, no returns, just silence. This makes them nearly impossible to win back through standard marketing channels.

### 6. Cohort Retention

Post-rebrand cohorts retain 6-7 percentage points worse at every milestone (M1, M3, M6, M12). Pre-rebrand cohorts also show a retention cliff immediately after the rebrand date, confirming that existing customers were disrupted.

---

## Recommendations

### Immediate Actions (0-3 months)

1. **Win-Back Campaign** — Target the "At Risk" and "Cant Lose Them" segments with personalized offers. Focus on 35-44 women with store channel preference. Estimated recoverable revenue: €800K-1.2M annually.

2. **Price Sensitivity Testing** — Run A/B tests on key categories to find the optimal price point that balances the new positioning with purchase frequency.

3. **Email Re-engagement** — Launch an opt-in campaign for the 68.6% of churned customers with no email engagement. Offer an incentive for resubscription.

### Medium-Term (3-6 months)

4. **Omnichannel Bridge Program** — Create a seamless store-to-online experience. Offer in-store customers an online account with personalized recommendations and exclusive digital content.

5. **Loyalty Program 2.0** — Redesign the loyalty program to reflect the new brand positioning while retaining value for existing tiers. Consider grandfather clauses for Gold/Platinum members.

### Long-Term (6-12 months)

6. **Segmented Communication Strategy** — Develop distinct messaging tracks for pre-rebrand customers (emphasize continuity) vs. post-rebrand customers (emphasize newness).

7. **Store Experience Refresh** — Invest in physical store updates that align with the new brand identity while maintaining elements of familiarity for existing customers.

---

## Methodology

- **RFM Segmentation:** Quintile-based scoring (1-5) on Recency, Frequency, and Monetary dimensions
- **Churn Definition:** No purchase within 180 days of data end date (2024-12-31)
- **Cohort Analysis:** Monthly acquisition cohorts tracked over 12-month retention windows
- **Tools:** Python (pandas, seaborn, matplotlib), SQL, Tableau
